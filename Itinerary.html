Sample Itinerary

A simple itinerary to visit Mammoth Cave!

About

Welcome to Mammoth Cave National Park! Let us tell you all about Mammoth Cave. There are over 400 miles of explored caverns and passageways. If you like to try new things, then there are plenty of excursions to go on. In Kentucky, under the surface of this great park, is a mind-boggling maze where you can easily get lost.

That is why you always explore with a trained guide. Under the surface, there is a layer of shale and a layer of limestone. These keep the water at the surface. However, in special places called sinkholes, water seeps underground and erodes the rock. This creates the caverns and shafts that are still being explored today.

There are only 10 miles out of the 400 miles that are available for touring. Approximately, 390,000 people visit the cave yearly. What is amazing about caves is two things. The first is the shapes that the cave walls, formations, stalagmites, or stalactites take. Second, thinking that just water created all of this beauty and nothing else. If all of this is of interest to you, just keep on reading and a five-day itinerary is coming up!

IMPORTANT THINGS TO KNOW

1. Never trust your GPS, or Google Maps when traveling to the park. If you need directions, visit this site.
2. All cave tours commence at the visitor center, unless specified.

DAY 1

  • 12:00 PM: Arrive and get lunch.

  • 2:00 PM: Travel to Campground Trail. Time: 0.5 mile, 8 minutes.

  • 2:15 PM: Hike at Campground Trail for 1 mile, 30 minutes. Description: This short path leaves near campsite 107, which is in the 92-111 loop of the campground. See lots of cedar trees and rock outcrops.

  • 2:45 PM: Travel to Dixon Cave Trail. Time: 0.5 mile, 10 minutes by walking.

  • 3:00 PM: Hike at Dixon Cave Trail and Green River Bluffs Trail. Total time: 3 miles, 1 hour and 30 minutes. Description: This short path begins at the Historic Entrance to Mammoth Cave and then ascends along a bluff line to pass Dixon Cave. You can then follow the Dixon Cave Connector uphill to the Headquarters Picnic Area or continue along the Green River Bluffs Trail.

  • 4:30 PM: Return to hotel.

  • 6:00 PM: Dinner at hotel.

  • 8:00 PM: Return to room.

  • 9:30 PM: Bedtime.

DAY 2

  • 8:30 AM: Wake Up!

  • 9:00 AM: Breakfast in hotel. Time: 1 hour.

  • 10:00 AM: Travel to the Domes and Dripstones Tour. Time: ¼ of a mile, 5 minutes.

  • 10:15 AM: Domes and Dripstones Tour. Time: 2 hours. Description of tour: Most stunning stalagmites and stalactites. There are dramatic pits and domes. You also get to see Frozen Niagara.

  • 12:15 PM: Travel to El Mazatlán #4. Time: 9.6 miles, 17 minutes.

  • 12:45: Have lunch at El Mazatlán #4. Time: 2 hours.

  • 2:45 PM: Travel to Blair Spring Hollow Trail. Time: 18.9 miles, 30 minutes.

  • 3:15 PM: Hike at Blair Spring Hollow Trail and Collie Ridge Trail. Total length: 7.4 miles, 3 hours. Description: The Blair Spring Hollow Trail links Collie Ridge Trail on the east with Wet Prong Buffalo Creek Trail to the west. It is one of the shortest backcountry trails in the park. There are no campsites along it.

  • 6:15 PM: Travel to Green River Grill (hotel restaurant). Time: 9.6 miles, 15 minutes.

  • 6:30 PM: Have dinner at hotel restaurant. Time: 2 hours.

  • 8:30 PM: Return to hotel room.

  • 9:30 PM: Bedtime.

DAY 3

  • 8:30 AM: Wake up!

  • 9:00 AM: Breakfast in hotel. Time: 1 hour.

  • 10:00 AM: Travel to the Historic Cave Tour. Time: ¼ mile, 5 minutes.

  • 10:15 AM: Historic Cave Tour. Time: 2 hours. Description: It is the classic tour and the most popular. It is also the famous tour taken by celebrated figures in the 1800s and 1900s. It goes through the Historic Entrance and you get to learn all about the history of Mammoth Cave.

  • 12:15 PM: Travel to El Mazatlán #4. Time: 9.6 miles, 17 minutes.

  • 12:45 PM: Have lunch at El Mazatlán #4. Time: 1.5 hours.

  • 2:15 PM: Travel to the Frozen Niagara Tour. Time: 9.6 miles, 17 minutes.

  • 2:40 PM: Frozen Niagara Tour. Time: 1 hour and 20 minutes. Description: It is remarkable. It is perfect for someone who wants to see something amazing without going far. It also features a breathtaking 75-foot waterfall.

  • 4:00 PM: Travel to Mammoth Cave Sink Dome Trail. Time: ¼ of a mile, 5 minutes.

  • 4:15 PM: Hike at Mammoth Cave Sink Dome Trail. Length: 2.7 mile. Time: 1 hour and 15 minutes. Description: Mammoth Cave Sink Dome Trail is a trail that features a cave. The elevation gain is 357 feet and the trail is good for all skill levels.

  • 5:30 PM: Travel back to hotel. Time: ¼ of a mile, 5 minutes.

  • 6:00 PM: Have dinner at hotel. Time: 2 hours.

  • 8:00 PM: Travel to the Star Chamber Tour. Time: ¼ mile, 5 minutes.

  • 8:15 PM: Star Chamber Tour. Time: 2 hours and 30 minutes. Description: It is a night tour. It goes through the Historic Entrance and part of the Violet Tour. Bring a flashlight!

  • 10:45 PM: Return to hotel. ¼ mile, 5 minutes.

  • 11:00 PM: Bedtime.

DAY 4

  • 8:30 AM: Wake up!

  • 9:00 AM: Breakfast in the hotel. Time: 1 hour.

  • 10:00 AM: Travel to the Cee Bee Food Store. Time: 13.4 miles, 20 minutes.

  • 10:20 AM: Shop for picnic and breakfast for Day 5. Time: 30 minutes.

  • 10:50 AM: Travel to Cedar Sink Trail. Time: 20 miles, 30 minutes.

  • 11:20 AM: Hike at Cedar Sink Trail and picnic there. Time: 1 hour and 50 minutes. Description: This trail is known for its spring wildflowers as well as its massive sinkholes-some of which in an area known as the “Land of the 10,000 Sinks”. The path has been renovated, with more observation decks built in the sink as well as another set of stairs added so hikers can now make a loop in and out of Cedar Sink.

  • 1:20 PM: Travel to the Grand Avenue Tour. Time: 10 minutes, 6.6 miles.

  • 1:30 PM: Grand Avenue Tour. Time: 4 hours and 30 minutes. Description: It is the second largest tour and it is perfect for a challenge. You should do the Historic Tour and the Domes and Dripstones Tour first. You must be over six years to participate.

  • 6:00 PM: Travel back to hotel. Time: ¼ mile, 5 minutes.

  • 6:30 PM: Dinner at hotel. Time: 1.5 hours.

  • 9:30 PM: Bedtime.

DAY 4

  • 9:00 AM: Wake up!

  • 9:30 AM: Make breakfast in hotel room.

  • 10:30 AM: Mammoth Cave Railroad Hike/Bike Trail. Length: 8.8 miles. Time: 1 hour and 30 minutes. Description: This bicycle trail mostly follows the old railroad grade connecting Park City to Mammoth Cave National Park and historic sites. Note: Make sure to bring hiking shoes and, if you are using bikes, make sure to take mountain bikes. Also, even if it follows the old railroad, it has some steep grades. You will probably need to dismount your bike in some areas.

  • 12:00 PM: Lunch at hotel. Time: 2 hours.

  • 2:00 PM: Return and pack.

  • 3:00 PM: Leave.
